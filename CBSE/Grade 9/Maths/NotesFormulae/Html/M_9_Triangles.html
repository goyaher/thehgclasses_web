Congruency:
• Two figures are congruent if they are of same size and shape i.e., they are identical in all respects.
• If two triangles ABC and PQR are congruent under the correspondence A  P, B  Q and C  R, then
symbolically, it is expressed as ∆ ABC  ∆ PQR.
• Congruency Rules for triangles –
o SSS (Side Side Side) – If three sides of one triangle are equal to the three sides of another
triangle
o SAS (Side Angle Side) – If two sides and included angle of one triangle are equal to the 2 sides
and included angle of another triangle
o ASA (Angle Side Angle) – If two angles and included side of one triangle are equal to the 2
angles and included side of another triangle
o AAS (Angle Angle Side) – If two angles and one side of one triangle are equal to the 2 angles and
one side of another triangle
o RHS (Right angle Hypotenuse Side) – If in two right triangles, hypotenuse and one side of one
triangle are equal to hypotenuse and one side of another triangle
• If two triangles are congruent, then corresponding parts are equal. In other words, Corresponding
Parts of Congruent Triangles (CPCT) are equal.
